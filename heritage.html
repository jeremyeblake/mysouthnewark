Our Heritage

Who is South End Newark?

The South End of Newark, Ohio is an area that is literally and figuratively made up of side streets. It is home to a variety of wonderful people, churches, and community organizations that have deep emotional ties in the area. These podcasts were produced by students enrolled in Denison University Professor Anne Crowley's “Technology & Learning” course. Veterans of Foreign Wars

The Veterans of Foreign Wars (VFW) is a veterans organization that strives for better health care and benefits on behalf of American veterans. Its missions include maintaining a nationwide organization that helps assisting veterans with their VA disability claims, employment and attracting volunteers. The VFW hall or patio space on Forry Street can rented for large events and gatherings.

John Clem

John Clem was one of the youngest Civil War heroes, born in 1851 in Newark. At nine years old, he ran away from home to enlist in the Union Army, but was turned down because he was so young. However, the army agreed to take him in as a drummer boy. He participated in many battles and became renowned for his bravery in combat. At twelve years old, he achieved the rank of sergeant, becoming the youngest noncommissioned officer to serve in the U.S. army. After rising to national fame, he served a long military career of almost fifty years before retiring. The Johnny Clem House, where many believe he lived as a boy, was at 26 W. Harrison Street in the South End.

Baseball

The sport of baseball is a rich part of our neighborhood. South Newark is fortunate to have many places of pride supporting baseball in our community. Mound City Little League was established in 1951 and offers a unique experience to any youth in Licking County. Don Edwards Park in Little Texas, the former home of the Babe Ruth World Series, is now the summer home of the Licking County Settlers, a collegiate league. There are many baseball fields throughout the neighborhood including Wells Park.

McGuffey Elementary

McGuffey Elementary, located in Newark, has been appointed by the Ohio Department of Education as a high-performing academics school. Being that, McGuffey’s main focus are the students and the parents; McGuffey has programs and events for the parents that provide opportunities for them to assist in planning, reviewing and offering suggestions through surveys and meetings.

Ben Franklin Elementary

Ben Franklin Elementary located in Newark, currently has 385 students enrolled, which range from Preschool to fifth grade. Furthermore, Ben Franklin is distinguished for their school-sponsored programs, such as the yearbook club, walking club, talent show, and student leadership. Along with this, the Boeing Corporation is another sponsor, which provides students and families with COSI events. By providing these programs, parents have the opportunity to get involved with their child’s education.

American Legion

The American Legion is a veterans organization that provides financial, social and emotional support to members of the Armed Forces, veterans and their families. The American Legion Post 85 is located at the intersection of South Sixth Street and Wilson Street.
